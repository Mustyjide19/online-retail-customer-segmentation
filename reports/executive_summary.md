# Executive Summary

This project analysed over 500,000 online retail transactions from a UK-based retailer to identify customer behaviour, revenue trends, product performance and retention opportunities.

After cleaning the raw dataset, the analysis identified approximately £8.91 million in revenue across 18,532 orders, 4,338 customers and 3,665 products.

The project utilised RFM analysis to segment customers into groups, including Champions, Loyal Customers, Big Spenders, At-Risk customers, Lost Customers, and Regular Customers.

Additional analysis included product ABC classification, customer value concentration, cohort retention analysis and bought-together product analysis. These methods helped identify high-value products, important customer groups, customer retention behaviour and cross-selling opportunities.

The findings can help the retailer improve customer retention, prioritise high-value products, target at-risk customers and create product bundle opportunities.
